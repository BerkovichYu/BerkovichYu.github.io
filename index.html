About me

My name in Chinese: 张世宇

Email: shiyu123@mail.ustc.edu.cn

I was born in 1999 in Chengtanjiang, a peaceful town through which the Nanchuan River (南川河) flows. I usually enjoy reading detective and science fiction novels, as well as watching their adaptations. When I'm alone, my headphones are always with me. I particularly love listening to rock and R&B. Recently, my favorite song is 'Inside Out' by Spoon.

Currently, I am a fifth-year Ph.D. student in Mathematics at the University of Science and Technology of China (USTC), where I completed my B.S. in June 2020. My research primarily focuses on complex geometry, specifically on understanding the geometry of complex analytic spaces.

Recently, I've developed an interest in Mochizuki's work on the non-Abelian Hodge correspondence for parabolic Higgs bundles and try to understand its proof.

[4] Compact K\"ahler manifolds with partially semi-positive curvature, (with X. Zhang), arxiv preprint:2504.13155.

Based on the ideas developed in the previous paper [2], we can establish the structure theorems for partially semi-positive curvature conditions. One of the most intriguing implications of this paper is the Hacon-M^ckernan's type question for k-semi-positive Ricci curvature. Of independent interests, we confirm a question of Ni that a compact K\"ahler manifolds with positive orthogonal Ricci curvature is rationally connected.

[3] The Miyaoka-Yau inequality on minimal K\"ahler spaces, (with C. Zhang and X. Zhang), arXiv preprint:2503.13365.

We extend the classical Miyaoka-Yau inequality to all minimal K\"ahler klt spaces. One key is the existence of orbifold modification by Ou for klt spaces. However, our argument also work for compact K\"ahler variety with canonical singularities without Ou's modification, and is indeed new even for the smooth case.

[2] On the structure of compact K\"ahler manifolds with nonnegative holomorphic sectional curvature, (with X. Zhang), arXiv preprint:2311.18779v4.

The main result is the establishment of a "pseudoeffective" version of Bochner-type theorem, which leads to a splitting of the tangent bundle. This is a key step of the structure theorems of HSC≥0 for the Kahler case obtained by Professor S. Matsumura recently.
